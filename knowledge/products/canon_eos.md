## Overview
Canon EOS is a camera system that includes both DSLR and mirrorless cameras, widely used for professional photography and videography.

## Key Features
- Mirrorless and DSLR models
- Interchangeable lenses (RF, EF mount)
- Advanced autofocus systems
- 4K video recording (in newer models)
- Wireless connectivity

## Common Issues
- Autofocus issues
- Overheating during video recording
- Connectivity problems (Wi-Fi/Bluetooth)
- Battery drain
- Firmware-related bugs

## Technical Specifications
- Sensor: APS-C / Full Frame
- Connectivity: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth
- Storage: SD cards
- Video: Full HD / 4K

## Troubleshooting Guide
### Autofocus Issues
- Update firmware
- Reset camera settings
- Clean lens and sensor

### Overheating
- Limit long recordings
- Turn off when not in use
- Avoid high ambient temperatures

### Connectivity Issues
- Reset Wi-Fi settings
- Re-pair device
- Update app

## Compatibility
- Compatible with Canon RF and EF lenses (adapter needed)
- Works with Canon mobile apps

## Best Practices
- Keep firmware updated
- Use high-speed SD cards
- Avoid prolonged overheating
