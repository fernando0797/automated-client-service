## Overview
Dyson vacuum cleaners are high-performance cleaning devices, often cordless, known for strong suction power and advanced filtration systems.

## Key Features
- Cyclone suction technology
- Cordless operation (most models)
- HEPA filtration (model dependent)
- Detachable battery
- Multiple cleaning attachments

## Common Issues
- Loss of suction power
- Battery not charging or draining quickly
- Brush bar not spinning
- Blockages in airflow
- Overheating shut-off

## Technical Specifications
- Power: Battery-powered or corded
- Battery life: ~20–60 minutes depending on mode
- Filtration: Multi-stage filtration system

## Troubleshooting Guide
### Loss of Suction
- Check for blockages in tube or filter
- Clean filters
- Empty dust bin

### Battery Issues
- Fully charge battery
- Replace battery if degraded
- Avoid storing in extreme temperatures

### Brush Bar Issues
- Remove tangled debris
- Check motorized head

## Compatibility
- Compatible with Dyson attachments
- Some models support interchangeable batteries

## Best Practices
- Clean filters regularly
- Avoid overfilling dust bin
- Store properly after use
